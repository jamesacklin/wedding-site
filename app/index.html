About Fallingwater

Fallingwater is a beautiful historic house designed by Frank Lloyd Wright in 1935 for the Kauffman family. The house is cantilevered dramatically over a waterfall—truly a sight to behold. Fallingwater was designated a National Landmark in 1966 and named on Smithsonian’s “Life List.”

We chose our venue because the Fallingwater house is our favorite place to visit. More than anything, we would love for our friends and family to experience this spectacular masterpiece first-hand. We strongly encourage you to visit Fallingwater during the day before our ceremony.

Other Attractions

The Pennsylvania Laurel Highlands are full of beautiful natural attractions.

  • Kentuck Knob, another Frank Lloyd Wright-designed residence
  • Fort Necessity National Battlefield, the site of an early battle of the French and Indian War
  • Ohiopyle Falls, a scenic overlook over a 20-foot waterfall that spans the Youghiogheny River
  • 79 miles of hiking trails in Ohiopyle State Park
  • Cycling on the Great Allegheny Passage trail
